Lab Information

Background

The Nepalese power grid is transitioning to a state of surplus generation, eliminating loadshedding and paving the way for the adoption of advanced technologies such as smart grids, digital substations, optical fiber communication for power systems, and phasor measurement units. These advancements aim to make the Integrated Nepal Power System (INPS) stronger, more reliable, and efficient.

The Grid Modernization Lab (GML) at Kathmandu University is dedicated to developing state-of-the-art tools and software to enhance the INPS. These tools are designed to be more accurate, computationally efficient, and robust compared to those currently used by the Nepal Electrical Authority (NEA) and Independent Power Producers (IPPs). Our research focuses on critical areas such as energy storage (pumped hydro and others), wide-area monitoring, and control systems, which are vital for the future of INPS. Additionally, GML collaborates with industry partners to develop technologies that reduce reliance on imported products.

Objectives

  • Develop tools using cutting-edge techniques to enhance the electric grid.
  • Demonstrate cutting edge technologies for grid enhancement through real world implementations.
  • Serve as a hub for academicians and industrialists in grid modernization.

Scope of the Lab

This lab aims to conduct research and provide tools to analyze the power system. Furthermore, it also aim to develop technologies that can strengthen the power systems. Thus, the scope of this project are as follows:

  • Application of cutting edge technologies such as artificial intelligence (AI), probability based methods etc for power systems.
  • Special focus on issues in Integrated Nepal Power Systems (INPS).

Development and Sustainability Plan

The GML lab will be developed in four stages: Stage I where the important research areas will be narrowed down, Stage II where the laboratory set-up will be done, Stage III will demonstrate the research and technologies capabilities of the lab followed by Stage IV where the lab will be advertised in various communication mediums to make it more visible in the power system field.
